Rivers Lab Research Workflow

Educational Psychology and Learning Support

Self-Regulation → Evidence → Modelling → Support → Improvement
1
Context

Student Learning Activity

Coursework, assignments, study routines, attendance, and classroom participation form the behavioural basis for analysis.

2
Constructs

Self-Regulation and Metacognition

Students monitor, plan, evaluate, and adjust learning through executive-function and metacognitive behaviours.

3
Evidence

Survey and Behavioural Data

Self-report instruments, LMS activity, assignment timing, learning records, and academic outcomes provide complementary evidence.

4
Processing

Data Preparation

Responses and behavioural records are cleaned, coded, linked, and prepared for quantitative analysis.

5
Analysis

Statistical Modelling

Regression, factor analysis, path analysis, and SEM clarify relationships among learning behaviours, regulation, and GPA.

6
Output

Learning Support Insight

Findings identify practical points for intervention, course design, advising, and student-facing feedback.

7
Improvement

Teaching and Support Adaptation

Evidence is translated into improved learning support, study-skills instruction, and targeted institutional practice.

Evidence loop Each stage converts student learning activity into interpretable evidence, then returns that evidence to teaching, advising, and support design.
Learning evidence loop — each cycle informs better support
